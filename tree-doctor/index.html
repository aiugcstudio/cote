Meet your arborist

A lifelong gardener, naturalist, and tree climber.

I am professionally certified as an arborist by the International Society of Arboriculture, and licensed by the Pennsylvania Department of Agriculture as a landscape pesticide applicator. I work as arborist, landscaper, and consultant, primarily for private residential clients, helping them care for their trees, shrubs, and gardens.

The Tree Doctor is a small business, fully owner-operated. I am usually present on jobs to supervise and help perform the work myself. I make regular communication with clients a centerpiece of my work, and strive to maintain long-term relationships with the people, and the trees, I am lucky enough to meet.

The approach

Diagnose, treat, and care.

Every tree gets the same considered method a good doctor brings to a patient. Look closely first, treat only what needs treating, then keep watching over the years.

01

Diagnose

A close look at the whole plant, its roots, and its site. Years of hands-on experience reading what insects, fungus, soil, and stress are actually doing, before anything is prescribed.

02

Treat

Targeted pruning, plant-health care, and pest management with the lightest touch that works. No dousing everything in chemicals, only what the tree genuinely needs to recover.

03

Care

Regular communication and long-term relationships with the people, and the trees, worth looking after. Check-backs weeks and seasons later to see how a treatment is holding.

The practice

All phases of tree care, from planting to pruning and removals.

A few things I have grown quite good at over the years, and love doing well.

Rx / 01

Tree & shrub pruning

My favorite activity. Good pruning improves plant health and property appearance like little else. If you know how you want your trees pruned, I can do it. If you don't, I can help you figure it out and leave them perfectly pruned on your behalf.

Rx / 02

Health & pest management

A licensed pesticide applicator with years of hands-on experience diagnosing tree and shrub conditions caused by insects, fungus, fertility, and other factors. If you need to know what is wrong with your tree and want to fix it without an excess of chemicals, I am one of your best bets.

Rx / 03

Risk assessments & removals

Reliable, impartial advice on whether a tree should be pruned, removed, or cabled for reasons of safety. I love trees, not so much money, and I am committed to preserving the forest canopy whenever possible, with honest advice always.

Before you call

Questions, answered.

A few things homeowners often ask before the first visit.

Do you charge for an initial look?+
Reach out with a little about your trees and we'll arrange a visit. I'd rather see the plant in person, understand what you're hoping for, and give you honest advice than guess from a distance.
Will you push a removal?+
No. I love trees, not so much money, and I'm committed to preserving the canopy whenever it's safe to do so. If a tree can be pruned, treated, or cabled instead of removed, that's the plan I'll recommend.
Are you licensed to apply treatments?+
Yes. I'm licensed by the Pennsylvania Department of Agriculture as a landscape pesticide applicator, and I use the lightest effective approach rather than dousing everything in chemicals.
Who actually does the work?+
The Tree Doctor is a small, fully owner-operated business. I'm usually on site to supervise and perform the work myself, and I make regular communication with clients a centerpiece of how I work.
Which areas do you serve?+
I'm based in Devon and care for trees, shrubs, and gardens across the surrounding Main Line, including Wayne, Berwyn, Paoli, Malvern, Radnor, Villanova, Bryn Mawr, and Strafford.
