The Science

Photobiomodulation, done properly.

Red light therapy is photobiomodulation, also called low-level light therapy. It uses non-UV red and near-infrared light thought to act on the mitochondria and cytochrome-c-oxidase, modulating nitric-oxide signaling, microcirculation, and collagen remodeling.

Professional systems pair red and near-infrared ranges to target both superficial and deeper tissue. Results follow a biphasic sweet spot: too little underperforms, too much can diminish the gains.

  • 630 to 660 nmRed wavelengths for superficial tissue
  • 810 to 850 nmNear-infrared for deeper penetration
  • 10 to 15 minTypical high-irradiance full-body session

How long are typical sessions?

Most clients enjoy full benefits in just 10 to 15 minutes, thanks to our high-irradiance LED beds.

Is red light therapy safe?

Yes. It's non-invasive, drug-free, and backed by decades of research. It is non-UV and generally well-tolerated when used as directed. Protect the eyes as instructed, and use caution with photosensitizing medications, light-sensitive conditions, open wounds, or pregnancy.
