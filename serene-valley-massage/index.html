Questions

Good to know.

What is manual lymphatic drainage?

A specialized, very light massage technique that stimulates the lymphatic system to move waste, toxins, and excess fluid out of the body. Gentle, rhythmic strokes follow the lymphatic pathways so fluid can be filtered and eliminated, easing swelling and supporting immunity.

How is craniosacral therapy different from a regular massage?

Craniosacral therapy uses very light pressure at key points, including the head, neck, and sacrum, to release tension and let cerebrospinal fluid flow more freely. It is gentle and hands-on rather than deep-tissue, and supports your body's own natural healing.

Can massage help after surgery or with lymphedema?

Yes. Manual lymphatic drainage is often used to support post-surgical recovery and to help manage lymphedema symptoms by reducing swelling and improving circulation. Phyllis is a certified manual lymphatic drainage therapist and will tailor the work to where your body is in recovery.

Do you accept insurance or workers' comp?

Serene Valley Massage is a DOL Workers' Comp and OPTUM VA-CCN insurance provider. Reach out with your details and we will let you know how your visit can be covered.

What should I expect at my first session?

We start by understanding what your body needs, then choose a style and modality that fits, whether that is lymphatic drainage, craniosacral therapy, or rehabilitation massage. Everything is gentle, intentional, and paced to you.
